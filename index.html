A Few Things

I'm Andy — a first-year engineering science student at U of T, and a musician who plays jazz on the side.

I approach design the way I approach making music: with practice, collaboration, and the kind of creativity that only comes when you know the rules well enough to bend them.

This portfolio is the record of my first year building things.

jazz structures biomedical praxis toronto eng. sci. · 2T8

Position Statement

I approach engineering design like I approach making music — with the application of practice, collaboration and creativity to express oneself.

"while the bridges I build collapse, I still approach design as applied creativity and an expression of my knowledge."

Three ideas carry this position across everything I build:

Ensemble. Jazz comes in a variety of ensembles. Musical sensitivity needs to be adapted to your ensemble size. I treat engineering teamwork the same way — listening to teammates share their perspective one by one, without skipping or judging.

Chord Changes. In jazz, the design space comes as the chord changes for a tune. Each tune has a specified set of chords which define restrictions. In Praxis, we learned to call this the design space. The fine-tuning of requirements matters, because there exists a sweet spot where creativity shines.

Vocabulary. There's no need to reinvent the alphabet. In jazz, vocabulary is standard — musicians transcribe the legends to build repertoire. In engineering, I start with failure equations and professor-given frameworks, then use that shoulder to reach higher.

— iteration on the Praxis I position statement. grade: 17.5/10.
